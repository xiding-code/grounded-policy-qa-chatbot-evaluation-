Returns to Third-Party Sellers
When you order from a third-party seller that fulfills and ships its own inventory,
your return is sent back to the seller instead of Amazon.

What to do next
Go to the
Amazon Online Returns Center
to start your return request for
third-party seller
items.
The seller will provide one of these return methods:
A return address in the United States
A prepaid return label
A full refund without returning the item
To pack your item for safe return to the seller:
Use the original packaging or a similar-sized box
Disassemble items that required assembly
Remove all personal information, passwords, and passcodes from electronic devices.
For instructions on how to do this, check the manufacturer’s website or contact the
manufacturer for support.
To return your item to the seller:
Use the shipping label provided by the seller
Items valued at $100 or more:
Insure the shipment and use a signature service
Items valued over $35:
Use a trackable shipping service
Items below $35:
We suggest using the USPS delivery confirmation service
We may not be able to cover you under the
A-to-z Guarantee
if:
The package doesn't arrive with the seller and you didn't use a trackable method to
return it, or
You refuse delivery of the shipment, to return it to the sender.
The seller will process your refund after receiving the return, usually within two
business days. You'll receive a notification from Amazon of your return and refund
details. Outbound shipping charges aren't always refunded upon return.
Refunds can be delayed if:
Your return is in transit longer than expected.
You use an untrackable shipping method.
The seller receives a different item than ordered.
An item that was sold as new, is returned with signs of use or damage.
A package is defined as extra large or heavy if it:
weights more than 150 pounds
is over 108 inches long
exceeds a total of 165 inches in length and girth [(2 x width) + (2 x height)].
For extra-large items, you'll receive either:
A postage-due label (you pay for shipping)
A prepaid label from the seller
Note:
Customers may be required to arrange drop-off at the carrier facility, even if the
seller provides a pre-paid shipping label.
If you need help after returning an item to a third-party seller:
You can
contact the seller
.
If the seller doesn't respond within 48 hours, you can
request an A-to-z Guarantee refund
The seller doesn't offer you an approved return method within 48 hours of asking to
return a damaged, defective, or not as described item, OR
You returned an item in line with
the Amazon Return Policy
, but you haven't received the expected refund. Check the
Return Fees
section of the Amazon Return Policy before requesting an A-to-z Guarantee refund.
